PHOENIX — Phenomenology Hub Of Emerging New Ideas, young researchers and eXploration

PHOENIX is an initiative by the Frascati National Laboratories (LNF) aimed at fostering future talent in phenomenology, with particular emphasis on topics aligned with LNF’s experimental research. It builds upon the legacy of the LNF Spring Institute organized by Gino Isidori and Luciano Maiani between 2005 and 2007, which played a pivotal role in shaping early-career theoretical physicists.

PHOENIX operates through a dedicated visiting program at LNF—mainly for young theoreticians—to cultivate a vibrant community engaged in discussions on particle physics phenomenology, with special focus on topics connected to the main experimental activities at the LNF.

Mission & Scope

Why PHOENIX?

PHOENIX catalyzes exchange between theory and experiment by hosting visiting researchers whose work resonates with the laboratory’s core activities. The goal is to foster rapid idea flow, critical discussion, and impactful collaborations within an open, supportive environment.

What We Focus On

  • Flavor physics and precision tests
  • Hadron structure and low-energy QCD
  • Searches for feeble interactions and dark sectors
  • Accelerator/beam physics & detector interfaces
  • Astroparticle/cosmology connections to lab programs

How We Work

  • Whiteboard sprints and focused mini-workshops
  • Open seminars and topical discussion forums
  • Match-making with local groups and experimental teams
  • Support for early-career researchers and mentors

Visitor Programs

Short-Stay Visitors (1–4 weeks from October to June)

Early-career visitors (PhD students and postdocs) and mid-career researchers pursuing projects aligned with LNF activities. Fellows deliver at least one seminar and engage in collaboration building with local groups.

  • Desk & IT access, seminar support
  • Collaboration matching with lab teams
  • Travel/housing support (subject to availability)

Targeted visits for joint papers, code/data sprints, or rapid exploration of new directions.

  • Whiteboard days & working sessions
  • Brown-bag talks; open problem clinics
  • Ideal for kick-starting new collaborations

Thematic Sprints & Mini-Workshops

One- to two-week focused programs around timely topics in phenomenology linked to LNF activities. Sprints culminate in open notes or brief reports for the community.

Day 1
Kickoff, goals, and problem scoping
Days 2–4
Working groups, whiteboard sessions, data/code jams
Day 5
Seminar & community discussion

How to Apply

Eligibility

  • Theoretical/phenomenology researchers at earlier career stages
  • Clear synergy with LNF’s experimental activities
  • Commitment to participate in PHOENIX seminars/discussions

What to Prepare

  • CV and publication list (PDF)
  • 1–2 page statement of purpose (project & LNF synergy)
  • Preferred dates & duration; desired collaborators
  • Reference(s) for early-career applicants

Process & Timeline

  • Rolling review; decisions typically within 4–6 weeks
  • Priority windows for thematic sprints
  • Support subject to availability & policies

Frequently Asked Questions

Is PHOENIX only for theoreticians?
The program is primarily aimed at phenomenologists and theorists, but we welcome applications from researchers whose work closely interfaces with LNF’s experimental efforts.

What support is available?
Depending on availability, PHOENIX can cover expenses for room in the guesthouse and lunches at the LNF canteen. Moreover, it can facilitate office space, seminar support, and collaboration matchmaking. Travel or housing support may be available—see the call details or contact us.

What are the expected outcomes?
Seminars, collaboration starts, working notes, or short reports from sprints. We value both concrete results and the creation of longer-term research links.

How far ahead should I apply?
Earlier is better—especially for visits requiring visa letters or peak-season accommodation. We recommend at least 8–12 weeks for international visitors.
