Mission & Scope

Why PHOENIX?

PHOENIX catalyzes exchange between theory and experiment by hosting visiting researchers whose work resonates with the laboratory‚Äôs core activities. The goal is to foster rapid idea flow, critical discussion, and impactful collaborations within an open, supportive environment.

What We Focus On

  • Flavor physics and precision tests
  • Hadron structure and low-energy QCD
  • Searches for feeble interactions and dark sectors
  • Accelerator/beam physics & detector interfaces
  • Astroparticle/cosmology connections to lab programs

How We Work

  • Whiteboard sprints and focused mini-workshops
  • Open seminars and topical discussion forums
  • Match-making with local groups and experimental teams
  • Support for early-career researchers and mentors

Frequently Asked Questions

Is PHOENIX only for theoreticians?
The program is primarily aimed at phenomenologists and theorists, but we welcome applications from researchers whose work closely interfaces with LNF‚Äôs experimental efforts.

What support is available?
Depending on availability, PHOENIX can facilitate office space, seminar support, and collaboration matchmaking. Travel or housing support may be available‚Äîsee the call details or contact us.

What are the expected outcomes?
Seminars, collaboration starts, working notes, or short reports from sprints. We value both concrete results and the creation of longer-term research links.

How far ahead should I apply?
Earlier is better‚Äîespecially for visits requiring visa letters or peak-season accommodation. We recommend at least 8‚Äì12 weeks for international visitors.
